# Telehealth vs. In-Person Care Outcomes Analysis

## Healthcare Data Analytics Case Study

## 📌 Project Summary

This project evaluates the effectiveness of **telehealth** compared with **traditional in-person healthcare visits** by integrating multiple healthcare datasets and applying statistical analysis to examine patient recovery, satisfaction, treatment costs, health literacy, technology access, and lifestyle factors.

## 🎯 Business Problem

Healthcare organizations continue to expand telehealth services, but understanding how virtual care compares with traditional in-person care remains essential.

This project evaluates patient recovery, satisfaction, treatment costs, health literacy, and technology access to support evidence-based healthcare decision-making.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

* Compare recovery time between telehealth and in-person visits.
* Analyze patient satisfaction across treatment methods.
* Evaluate treatment cost patterns.
* Assess the impact of health literacy on patient outcomes.
* Explore the relationship between technology access and healthcare outcomes.

---

## 📂 Dataset Overview

The project integrates five healthcare datasets:

* Patient Clinical Data
* Employment & Insurance
* Device & Internet Access
* Health Literacy & Communication
* Lifestyle & Wellness

## 📊 Key Findings

* Telehealth patients recovered approximately **16 days faster** than those receiving in-person care.
* Higher health literacy was associated with improved recovery and patient satisfaction.
* Treatment cost alone was not a reliable predictor of recovery outcomes.
